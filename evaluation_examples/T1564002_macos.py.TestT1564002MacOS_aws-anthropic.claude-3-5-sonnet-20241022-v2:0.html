i'm creating lots of accounts for our macOS systems, and i want to hide the administrative accounts from being listed to the primary user. how do i do that?

LLM Generation

Query

i'm creating lots of accounts for our macOS systems, and i want to hide the administrative accounts from being listed to the primary user. how do i do that?

Output

To hide administrative accounts from being displayed on the macOS login screen and System Preferences, you can use one of these methods: 1. Using Terminal command: 

1
sudo dscl . create /Users/adminusername IsHidden 1
Replace "adminusername" with the actual username you want to hide. 2. If you want to hide multiple accounts, you can create a list in /private/var/db/dslocal/nodes/Default/users/.hidden: 
1
sudo echo "adminusername" >> /private/var/db/dslocal/nodes/Default/users/.hidden
To also hide the home directory of the hidden user: 
1
sudo chflags hidden /Users/adminusername
Note: These hidden accounts will still be accessible by typing their username at the login screen, but they won't be visible in the user list. The accounts remain fully functional; they're just not displayed. To reverse this: 
1
sudo dscl . delete /Users/adminusername IsHidden
Remember to replace "adminusername" with the actual username in all commands.
