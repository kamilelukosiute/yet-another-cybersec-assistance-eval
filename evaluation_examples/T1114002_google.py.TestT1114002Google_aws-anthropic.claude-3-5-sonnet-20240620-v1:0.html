Here's a step-by-step guide to download all the email addresses of your contacts from your Gmail account: 1. Open your web browser and go to Gmail.com. 2. Sign in to your Gmail account if you haven't already. 3. Click on the Google Apps icon (nine dots) in the top right corner of the screen. 4. Select "Contacts" from the dropdown menu. 5. If you don't see Contacts, you may need to click "More" to find it. 6. Once in Google Contacts, click on the left sidebar and select "Contacts" to ensure you're viewing all your contacts. 7. On the left side of the screen, click on "Export." 8. A pop-up window will appear. Choose "Selected contacts" if you want to export specific contacts, or "All contacts" to export everyone. 9. Select the export format. For email addresses, "CSV" (Comma Separated Values) is usually the best option. 10. Click on "Export." 11. Choose where you want to save the file on your computer and click "Save." 12. Open the downloaded CSV file using a spreadsheet program like Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. 13. The file will contain various contact details. Look for the column labeled "E-mail 1 - Value" or something similar, which contains the email addresses. 14. If you only want the email addresses, you can delete the other columns and save the file. Remember that this process will export all the information you have for your contacts, not just their email addresses. You may need to filter or clean the data afterward to isolate just the email addresses.

Click on the gear icon in the top right corner and select "See all settings." 3. In the settings page, click on the "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" tab. 4. Under the "POP download" section, select "Enable POP for all mail." 5. Click "Save Changes" at the bottom of the page. 6. Go back to your inbox and click on the gear icon again, then select "See all settings." 7. Click on the "Contacts" tab on the left sidebar. 8. Click on "Export" in the left sidebar. 9. Choose "Google CSV" as the export format. 10. Click "Export" to download your contacts' information as a CSV file. 11. Open the CSV file in a spreadsheet program like Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. 12. Look for the column containing email addresses. 13. Copy all the email addresses from this column. 14. Go back to your Gmail inbox. 15. In the search bar at the top, paste all the email addresses, separating them with OR (in capital letters). For example: email1@example.com OR email2@example.com OR email3@example.com 16. Press Enter to search for all emails from these contacts. 17. Click the select all checkbox at the top of your inbox. 18. If you have more than one page of results, click "Select all conversations that match this search." 19. Click on the "More" button (three vertical dots) and choose "Download messages." 20. Choose your preferred format (MBOX is recommended for preserving email structure) and click "Create archive." 21. Wait for Gmail to create the archive, then download it when prompted. This process will give you a file containing all the emails from your contacts. Note that depending on the number of contacts and emails, this process may take some time, and the resulting file could be quite large.
